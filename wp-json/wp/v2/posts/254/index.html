I was searching for IoT Blockchain projects a while ago, and I found IoteX<\/a><\/span><\/p>\n

I was keeping an eye on the company and its projects. One morning, I woke up with hype for one new announcement they were going to launch,\u00a0 <\/span>Machinefi.<\/span><\/p>\n

I quickly opened their vision paper and started to deep dive on it; what I discovered was way beyond impressive; it was eye-opening, their vision paper raised the question:\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

<\/span><\/h1>\n

Who will own the future workforce, “machines,” and therefore the economy?<\/span><\/h1>\n

<\/span><\/p>\n

Machines are already taking a significant part of work in different sectors, and this path is only going up, increasing year by year, some predictions:<\/span><\/h2>\n
    \n
  1. McKinsey: By 2030, machines will outnumber humans by 10:1 and generate 12 trillion dollars in economic value.<\/span><\/li>\n
  2. PwC: 50% of all jobs will be partially automated by 2030.<\/span><\/li>\n
  3. Deloitte: 30% of the world’s workers will be replaced entirely by machines within 20 years.<\/span><\/li>\n<\/ol>\n

    <\/span><\/p>\n

    Beyond predictions, Machinefi’s vision, in a few words: break the status quo<\/strong> instead of corporations owning the world, therefore the machines; they enable people and communities to be drivers of this machine economy.<\/strong><\/span><\/p>\n

     <\/p>\n

    One more note: Remember, machines could be physical and \/ or digitals, with different levels of intelligence and acting over the real world and \/ or the digital world.<\/span><\/p>\n

    I invite you to read their vision paper.<\/span><\/p>\n
